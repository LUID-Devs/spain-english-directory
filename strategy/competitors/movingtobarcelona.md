## Executive Summary

**MovingToBarcelona** is a **Barcelona-only expat resource** combining a relocation guide blog with an English-speaking services directory. Founded by UK expats, it offers first-hand advice for British and English-speaking expats moving to Barcelona from the UK.

**Key Finding:** MovingToBarcelona is **Barcelona-only** (single city coverage) with a **limited directory** (~20-30 listings) that appears to be under construction. Their strength is authentic expat experience content; their weakness is minimal directory coverage and no multi-city expansion.

## Key Takeaways

1. **MovingToBarcelona is a single-city, small-scale competitor.** Their ~20-30 listings pose no threat to a comprehensive directory.

2. **Barcelona-only = market limitation.** They cannot serve the 80%+ of Spanish expats living outside Barcelona.

3. **Authentic voice is their strength.** First-hand expat experience builds trust; SED should incorporate user reviews for similar authenticity.

4. **Under-construction directory.** Their directory appears incomplete, suggesting resource constraints.

5. **Free model = no revenue barrier.** Both SED and MTB offer free access; competition is on comprehensiveness and features.

6. **UK-centric audience.** They specifically target British expats; SED has broader appeal to all English-speakers.

7. **No user engagement.** No reviews, forums, or community features; opportunity for SED to build network effects.

8. **Content + Directory hybrid.** MTB combines blog content with directory; SED should emulate this for SEO and user value.

## Conclusion

MovingToBarcelona represents a **single-city, limited-scope competitor** with authentic expat content but minimal directory coverage.
