## Executive Summary

SpainMadeSimple appears to operate as a broad expat-information platform that combines practical relocation content with business listings and referral pathways. Their model is less focused on verification and more focused on discoverability and partner visibility.

For Spain English Directory, the key opportunity is to compete on **quality of provider verification**, **service-level clarity**, and **city-specific filtering**, rather than matching high-volume generic content.

---

## Scope and Method

This document captures directional competitor insights from publicly visible site sections and category structure at analysis time (captured March 7, 2026).

What this report is not:

## Observed Positioning

### SpainMadeSimple strengths

- Broad expat-oriented informational content
- Multi-topic coverage that includes relocation-adjacent services
- Familiar "guide + listings" structure that can capture top-of-funnel users

### SpainMadeSimple weaknesses

- Limited evidence of strict language-verification workflow for listings
- Mixed intent pages (content + directory + partner promotion) can reduce trust for high-stakes services
- Lower transparency on provider quality standards versus a verification-first directory model

## Strategic Implications

1. **Do not compete on generic breadth alone.**
   Compete on decision quality in high-friction categories.

2. **Verification-first is a moat.**
   Providers should be scored by language confidence, service scope clarity, and response reliability.

3. **City-first UX beats generic list pages.**
   Expats search by city + service + urgency. Category pages should prioritize this path.

4. **Trust signals must be explicit.**
   Show verification date, confidence level, and evidence notes for each listing.
