# Expat Info Desk Competitor Analysis

## Executive Summary

**Expat Info Desk** (expatinfodesk.com) is an established global expat relocation platform founded in the early 2000s. It offers written city guides and a business directory across 34+ countries. For Spain specifically, it represents a significant competitive gap opportunity—covering only 2 of the top 5 expat cities.

## Business Model

### Revenue Streams

1. **Guide Sales** - Digital relocation guides ($15-25 each)
2. **Directory Listings**:
   - Basic: Free (searchable listing)
   - Pro: $300/year (enhanced description, logo, links, priority placement)
3. **Banner Advertising**:
   - Homepage top: $700/month
   - Side banner: $350/month
   - Bottom banner: $400/month

### Editorial Independence Policy

> "All advice and information provided within each of our destination guides is completely independent. We do not accept advertising or payment of any kind in return for positive coverage in those guides."

This separation between editorial and advertising is a key trust signal.

## Content Structure

Each guide follows a standardized 10-section template:

1. **Expat Accommodation** - Housing, neighborhoods, renting
2. **Legal Requirements** - Visas, permits, taxes, banking
3. **Education & Child Care** - Schools, nurseries, universities
4. **Transport** - Public transit, driving, car ownership
5. **Keeping in Touch** - Mobile, internet, media
6. **Shopping** - Retail, supermarkets
7. **Entertainment & Leisure** - Restaurants, nightlife, sports
8. **Health & Beauty** - Medical facilities, fitness, wellness
9. **Culture & Customs** - Language, traditions, etiquette
10. **Living Cost Comparison** - Budget guidance

### Healthcare Section Detail
- Two-tier system explanation (public vs private)
- Hospital directories with contacts
- Typical costs (~€90/private doctor visit)
- Emergency procedures

## Key Takeaways

1. **Expat Info Desk is content-first, directory-second.** Their guides are the core product; directory is secondary revenue.

2. **Major Spain coverage gap.** Only 2 of 5 top expat cities is a strategic oversight.

3. **Underdeveloped directory.** 35 total listings with empty categories shows lack of focus.

4. **No verification system.** Self-reported "English-speaking" without validation is exploitable.

5. **Technology gap.** Dated platform can't compete with modern UX.

6. **Community opportunity.** No reviews, forums, or events—expats want connection.
