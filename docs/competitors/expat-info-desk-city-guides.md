# Expat Info Desk City Guides Analysis

> Competitor research report for Spain English Directory  
> Issue: #1344 | Date: March 2026

## Executive Summary

Expat Info Desk (expatinfodesk.com) is a long-standing expat relocation guide platform offering city-specific guides written "by expats, for expats." They have two Spain city guides: **Barcelona** and **Madrid**. This analysis examines their content strategy, depth, and identifies gaps we can exploit.

---

## 1. Overview of Expat Info Desk

### Business Model
- **Type**: Paid relocation guides (premium content model)
- **Positioning**: "Written by an expat, for an expat" - emphasizing real lived experience
- **Coverage**: 30+ cities across 6 continents
- **Content Promise**: Comprehensive, regularly updated, independent (no advertising)

### Spain Coverage
| City | URL | Status |
|------|-----|--------|
| Barcelona | `/destinations/barcelona/` | Active |
| Madrid | `/destinations/madrid/` | Active |

**Note**: They only cover Barcelona and Madrid - no other Spanish cities (Valencia, Seville, Malaga, etc.)

## 3. Content Quality Assessment

### Strengths
1. **Personal Voice**: Content is written from personal experience with a conversational tone
2. **Practical Advice**: Real-world tips (e.g., "Be ready to call the owner as soon as you see an advertisement")
3. **Specific Numbers**: Actual price ranges for rent, transport costs
4. **Visa Detail**: Comprehensive visa information with official requirements vs "unofficial" reality
5. **Cultural Context**: Notes on Catalan vs Spanish language in Barcelona

### Weaknesses
1. **Incomplete Coverage**: Many promised sections return 404 or don't exist
2. **No Neighborhood Deep Dives**: Missing granular neighborhood guides
3. **Dated Information**: Some content references old data (2007 bike program launch, economic crisis references)
4. **No Visual Content**: No maps, photos, or infographics
5. **Limited Updates**: Some content appears stale
