# Spain English Proficiency Statistics Research

**Issue:** #1390  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Topic:** Verifying the claim that "only 5% of Spanish citizens speak English"

---

## Executive Summary

The claim that **"only 5% of Spanish citizens speak English" appears to be INCORRECT** based on official statistics. According to multiple authoritative sources:

- **Eurobarometer 2012**: 22.04% of Spaniards speak English (10.4 million people)
- **EF English Proficiency Index 2025**: Spain ranks #36 globally with "Moderate" proficiency
- **EF EPI Score**: 540 (above global average of 488)

The 5% figure significantly underestimates actual English proficiency in Spain. However, there ARE significant regional, generational, and professional variations that create the *perception* of limited English availability.

## Where Does the "5%" Claim Come From?

While official statistics show ~22% English proficiency, the perception of limited English may stem from:

### 1. Generational Divide
- **Older Spaniards (55+):** English proficiency is significantly lower
- **Younger Spaniards (under 35):** Much higher proficiency due to modern education
- Many professionals in key service roles (healthcare, bureaucracy) are older

### 2. Functional vs. Conversational English
- Many Spaniards have **basic English education** but lack confidence to use it
- "Can read English" ≠ "Can hold a conversation in English"
- Eurobarometer measures self-reported ability, which may include basic comprehension

### 3. Regional Variations
- Tourist areas (Costa del Sol, Barcelona, Madrid) have higher visible English usage
- Rural and interior regions have lower English penetration
- Expats may encounter limited English in specific contexts (public healthcare, government offices)

### 4. Professional Context Matters
- **Tourism industry:** High English proficiency
- **Public sector:** Lower English proficiency
- **Healthcare:** Mixed—older doctors often have limited English
- **Legal/Financial services:** Varies significantly
