# Expat Info Desk Competitor Gap Analysis

## Overview

**Expat Info Desk** (expatinfodesk.com) is an established expat relocation guide platform offering comprehensive city guides and a business directory for expatriates. Founded in the early 2000s, it positions itself as a written-by-expats-for-expats resource with a unique editorial independence promise.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
**Founded** | Early 2000s (established presence 20+ years)
| **Primary Language** | English
| **Geographic Focus** | Global (31+ city guides across 34+ countries)
| **Primary Format** | Written relocation guides + Business directory
| **Target Audience** | Expatriates, global nomads, international assignees
| **Spain Coverage** | 2 cities (Barcelona, Madrid)

---

## Business Model

Expat Info Desk operates on a **dual revenue model** with strict editorial separation:

### Revenue Streams

1. **Relocation Guide Sales**
   - Comprehensive city guides sold as digital products
   - Written by expats living in each destination
   - Regularly updated content

2. **Business Directory Listings**
   - **Basic Plan**: Free basic presence (searchable by country/category)
   - **Pro Plan**: $300 USD / €270 Euro annually
     - Enhanced description and contact information
     - Logo and links
     - Sponsored post classification (Google indexed)
     - Priority placement above basic listings

3. **Banner Advertising**
   - Top banner: $700 USD / €630 Euro per month (homepage)
   - Side banner (300x250): $350 USD / €320 Euro per month
   - Bottom banner: $400 USD / €360 Euro per month
   - Select page banner: $300 USD / €270 Euro per month

### Editorial Independence Policy

> "All advice and information provided within each of our destination guides is completely independent. We do not accept advertising or payment of any kind in return for positive coverage in those guides but may do so for companies that feature within this directory."

This separation is a key trust signal—they clearly distinguish between editorial content and paid directory listings.

- No community feedback on listings

- Limited interactivity

### 5. **No City-Specific Directory Filtering**

- Directory is global/national only
- Cannot filter for "Barcelona-only" or "Madrid-only" services
- Services listed may not actually serve specific cities

### 6. **Limited Spanish Ecosystem Understanding**

- Guide content is generic expat advice
- Limited understanding of Spain-specific nuances (e.g., autonomous community differences)
- No coverage of regional languages (Catalan, Valenciano, Gallego, Euskera)
- Missing local integration tips

### 7. **Premium Pricing Without Premium Features**

- Basic description and contact info
- Logo upload
- Link inclusion (marked as sponsored)
- Priority placement

**Missing features that justify premium pricing:**
- No review system
- No booking integration
- No analytics for listing owners
- No photo galleries
- No map integration
- No "verified English-speaking" badge

## Key Takeaways

1. **Expat Info Desk is a content company with a directory attached, not a directory-first platform.** Their guides are their core product; the directory is secondary revenue.

2. **They've left the door open in Spain.** Only covering Barcelona and Madrid while ignoring Valencia, Málaga, and Alicante—the top 3 expat cities—is a massive strategic gap.

3. **Their directory is underdeveloped.** 35 total listings with 4 empty categories shows lack of focus on this revenue stream.

4. **No verification = no trust.** Self-reported "English-speaking" status without validation is a weakness we can exploit with rigorous verification.

5. **Technology gap.** Their dated platform can't compete with modern UX expectations.

6. **Community opportunity.** No reviews, no forums, no events—expats want connection, not just information.

7. **Pricing without value.** $300/year for a basic listing is hard to justify without modern features.

## Conclusion

Expat Info Desk represents both a competitive threat and a massive opportunity. While they have brand recognition and 20+ years of SEO authority, their Spain coverage is surprisingly weak—only 2 cities despite Spain being a top 3 global expat destination.

Their directory model shows what's possible (paid listings at $300/year) but also what not to do (empty categories, no verification, no reviews). The gap in Valencia, Málaga, and Alicante coverage is a strategic gift that Spain English Directory can exploit.

**The playbook**: Launch in EID's blind spots (Valencia → Málaga → Alicante), build community and verification features they lack, then compete directly in Barcelona and Madrid with superior technology and deeper local expertise.
