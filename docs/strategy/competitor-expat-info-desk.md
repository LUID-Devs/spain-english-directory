# Expat Info Desk Competitor Analysis

**Source:** https://www.expatinfodesk.com/  
**Issue:** #1344 - Competitor Gap: Expat Info Desk City Guides  
**Analysis Date:** March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Expat Info Desk (EID) is an established expat relocation platform offering comprehensive city guides and a business directory. Founded in the early 2000s, it positions itself as a "written by expats, for expats" resource with guides for 31+ cities across 34+ countries.

**Key Finding for Spain:** EID only covers **2 of the top 5 expat cities** in Spain (Barcelona and Madrid), missing Valencia (#1), Málaga (#2), and Alicante (#3) completely.

### Guide Content Categories

Each city guide follows a standardized structure with the following sections:

#### 1. **Visas & Legal**
- Visa types and requirements
- Residency permits
- NIE application process
- Schengen area information
- EU vs non-EU citizen guidance

**Example URL Pattern:** `/destinations/[city]/visas/`

#### 2. **Accommodation**
- Renting vs buying guidance
- Residential area profiles
- Property market overview
- Where to live recommendations
- Metro/neighborhood guides

**Example URL Pattern:** `/destinations/[city]/accommodation/`  
**Sub-section:** `/destinations/[city]/accommodation/where-to-live/`

**Madrid Coverage Includes:**
- Historic center living (Sol, Gran Via, Chueca, Retiro)
- Metro station recommendations
- Price ranges (studios €500-700, 1-bed €750-900, 2-bed €800-1100)
- Agency vs direct owner guidance

**Barcelona Coverage Includes:**
- Barrio Gotico and Gaudi areas
- Long-term lease information (2-5 years)
- Mortgage availability for expats
- Abogado (lawyer) recommendations
- Links to US Consulate lawyer lists

#### 3. **Food & Dining**
- Traditional cuisine overview
- Meal timing customs
- Tapas culture
- International food availability
- Restaurant recommendations

**Example URL Pattern:** `/destinations/[city]/food/`

**Madrid Food Content:**
- Traditional dishes: callos, oreja, cocido madrileño
- Tapas and tapeo culture
- International cuisine (Moroccan, Turkish, Chinese, Indian, Japanese, Latin American)
- Breakfast, lunch, dinner customs

**Barcelona Food Content:**
- Catalan cuisine specifics
- Pan con tomate, trinxat, crema Catalana
- Ferran Adria/El Bulli influence
- Seafood and paella guidance
- Siesta timing and late dining

#### 4. **Communication**
- TV guide options
- Internet/mobile providers
- English-language resources
- Local blog recommendations

**Example URL Pattern:** `/destinations/[city]/communication/tv/`

#### 5. **Traditions & Culture**
- Tipping customs
- Local traditions
- Cultural customs to avoid
- Regional differences

**Example URL Pattern:** `/destinations/[city]/traditions/tipping/`

## Weaknesses & Gaps

### 1. **Severe Geographic Gaps in Spain**

| InterNations 2024 Ranking | City | EID Coverage |
|--------------------------|------|--------------|
| #1 | Valencia | ❌ **MISSING** |
| #2 | Málaga | ❌ **MISSING** |
| #3 | Alicante | ❌ **MISSING** |
| #7 | Madrid | ✅ Covered |
| #21 | Barcelona | ✅ Covered |

**Critical Gap:** Missing 60% of the top 5 Spanish expat destinations.

### 2. **No Vetting Process for Service Providers**

- Self-reported "English-speaking" status
- No verification of language capability
- No quality assessment
- No user review system

### 3. **Sparse Directory Listings**

- Only ~35 total listings across ALL categories for Spain
- 48% of listings are US tax-related (over-concentrated)
- 4 major categories have zero listings
- Many empty categories advertised but not populated

### 4. **Outdated Information**

- Some resource links are dated
- Website design shows age
- Limited mobile optimization
- No interactive features

### 5. **No City-Specific Directory Filtering**

- Directory is global/national only
- Cannot filter for "Barcelona-only" or "Madrid-only" services
- Services listed may not actually serve specific cities

### 6. **Limited Regional Understanding**

- No coverage of regional languages (Catalan, Valenciano, Gallego, Euskera)
- Limited understanding of autonomous community differences
- Generic expat advice rather than Spain-specific nuance

## Content Categorization Insights

### What EID Does Well

1. **Structured hierarchy**: `/destinations/[city]/[category]/[subcategory]/`
2. **Consistent sections** across all city guides
3. **Practical advice**: Cost ranges, metro stations, specific neighborhoods

## Recommendations for Spain English Directory

### 1. **Launch Valencia-First**

Expat Info Desk's #1 missing city is our #1 opportunity. Valencia is:
- Ranked #1 expat city (InterNations 2024)
- Zero competition from EID
- Large English-speaking expat community
- Underserved by existing directories

### 2. **Implement Rigorous Vetting**

While EID claims to list English-speaking services, they offer no verification. Our differentiator:
- Personal phone/email verification
- "English Verified" badge system
- Language proficiency ratings
- Video introductions

### 3. **Build Community from Day One**

EID has no community features. Add:
- User reviews and ratings
- Q&A forums per city
- Expat events calendar
- "Ask an expat" feature

### 4. **Modern Technology Stack**

EID's platform shows age. Differentiate with:
- Mobile-first responsive design
- Fast search with filters
- Map integration
- Click-to-call/WhatsApp integration
- Online booking where applicable

### 5. **Pricing Strategy**

EID charges $300/year for basic directory listings. Our approach:

| Tier | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Free** | €0 | Basic listing (name, address, phone) |
| **Basic** | €9/month | Description, photos, website link |
| **Premium** | €29/month | Priority placement, reviews widget, analytics |
| **Featured** | €99/month | Homepage placement, social promotion |

This undercuts EID while offering more value at each tier.

## Key Takeaways

1. **EID is a content-first, directory-second platform** - Their guides are their core product

2. **Major geographic gap in Spain** - Only 2 cities covered despite Spain being a top 3 expat destination

3. **No verification system** - Self-reported English-speaking claims without validation

4. **Outdated technology** - 20-year-old platform with limited modern features

5. **Pricing without value** - $300/year for basic listings without reviews, analytics, or booking

6. **Community opportunity** - No reviews, forums, or events despite expats wanting connection

---

## Conclusion

Expat Info Desk demonstrates both the opportunity and the gaps in the Spain expat market. While they have established authority and comprehensive written guides for Barcelona and Madrid, they completely miss Valencia, Málaga, and Alicante—the top 3 expat cities in Spain.

Their directory model shows what's possible ($300/year listings) but also what not to do (empty categories, no verification, no reviews). The lack of vetting for "English-speaking" service providers is a critical gap we can exploit.

**Our Strategy:** Launch in EID's blind spots (Valencia → Málaga → Alicante), build verification and community features they lack, then compete directly in Barcelona and Madrid with superior technology and deeper local expertise.
